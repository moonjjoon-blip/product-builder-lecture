Guide

What makes this page more useful than a bare test

The goal is not just to output a label. The page also explains how the result works, what affects it, and how your image is handled.

How It Works

The model compares two face-vibe categories

This tool looks at a face image and estimates whether the current impression reads closer to a puppy face or a cat face. The result is a probability-style summary, not a fixed truth.

Accuracy Tips

Front-facing shots and clear lighting matter

Side angles, backlight, aggressive filters, and heavy face occlusion can destabilize the output. A sharp image with visible eyes and face contour usually produces the cleanest prediction.

Privacy

Your image stays in the browser session

The page does not require an account to run the test, and the selected image is not stored on a site server. More detail is documented on the site info page.

Result Reading

How to interpret the output

  • A higher score means the model found stronger signals for that face vibe in the current image.
  • Live webcam predictions can shift slightly as your expression and angle change.
  • Uploaded photo results are strongly influenced by framing, lighting, and image quality.
  • The same person can receive a different result across photos, so the tool is best used for entertainment and light self-exploration.

FAQ

Common questions

Understanding the scope and limitations of the tool helps the result make sense.

Is this a scientific or medical diagnosis?

No. This is an entertainment-style AI classifier for face vibe categories and does not provide medical, psychological, or identity judgment.

Why can the result change from photo to photo?

Expression, lighting, camera angle, makeup, hair, and filter use all change the visual signals the model sees.

Where does the uploaded photo go?

The analysis runs in the browser, and the chosen image is not stored on the site server.

Is webcam permission safe to grant?

The browser permission applies to your current session and can be revoked at any time in your browser settings.

How do I get a more stable result?

Use a front-facing image, even lighting, a simple background, and a face that is not covered by objects or strong effects.
